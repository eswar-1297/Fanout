## What is This Tool?

The Fanout Query Generator helps your content team create **comprehensive, well-researched content** by breaking down any topic into multiple research angles and sub-topics.

## How to Use It - Step by Step

### Step 1: Enter Your Main Topic

In the "Main Query" field, enter the topic you want to create content about:

**Good Examples:**
- "What is product-market fit and how do I achieve it?"
- "How to start a successful podcast in 2024?"
- "Benefits and risks of intermittent fasting"
- "How does blockchain technology work?"
- "Best practices for email marketing campaigns"

**Tips:**
- Be specific but not too narrow
- Frame as a question when possible
- Include context if relevant (year, industry, etc.)

### Step 2: Set Domain (Optional)

Help the AI understand your content's context:

- **technical** - For developer docs, tech tutorials
- **marketing** - For business, marketing, sales content
- **health** - For wellness, fitness, medical content
- **research** - For academic, scientific content
- **general** - For consumer-facing content

### Step 3: Choose Max Fanouts

- **5-8 queries**: Quick blog post or social content
- **10-12 queries**: Comprehensive article or guide
- **15-20 queries**: Ultimate guide or pillar content

### Step 4: Generate & Review

Click "Generate Fanout Queries" and review the results.

**Look for:**
- ‚úÖ Are all important angles covered?
- ‚úÖ Any surprising angles you didn't think of?
- ‚úÖ Queries that match what your audience searches

### Step 5: Export Your Content Brief

Click **"Content Brief"** button to download a formatted document with:
- All queries organized by category
- Content coverage plan
- Checklist for writers
- Research query list

## Content Creation Workflow

### Workflow 1: Single Comprehensive Article

1. Generate fanout queries for your topic
2. Export as Content Brief

### Workflow 2: Content Series

1. Generate fanout queries for broad topic

3. Link them together as a content series

### Workflow 3: Content Gap Analysis

1. Generate fanout queries for topics you already covered

3. Identify gaps (queries not covered)
4. Create new content to fill gaps
5. Result: Complete topic coverage

## Common Questions

### Q: Do I need to use ALL the generated queries?
**A:** No! Use the queries that fit your content goals. Some might be redundant or not relevant to your audience.

### Q: Can I edit the queries?
**A:** Absolutely! The generated queries are starting points. Adapt them to match your brand voice and audience.

### Q: How often should I use this tool?
**A:** Use it for every major piece of content, new content series, or when planning quarterly content calendars.

### Q: What if the queries seem too basic or advanced?
**A:** Adjust your main query to include level indicators: "beginner's guide to..." or "advanced techniques for..."

### Q: Can I use this for social media content?
**A:** Yes! Generate queries, then turn each into a social post, thread, or series.
